A catchment defining a fundamental subdivision of the landscape in which water can be modelled as draining to a single outlet, to an adjacent waterbody, or internally to an area devoid of waterbody features. An elementary catchment is bounded by other elementary catchments; that is, elementary catchments compose a complete coverage. Four types of elementary catchments are recognized: reach catchments, bank catchments, empty catchments, and water catchments. An elementary catchment is generally equivalent to a reach catchment in the US NHDPlus.
